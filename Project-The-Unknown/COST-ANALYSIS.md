# Cost-Benefit Analysis

## Overview
This document compares the cost of our AWS cloud solution against the traditional approach of hiring additional staff to manage the restaurant's growing demand.

## Cost Comparison

### Option 1: AWS Cloud Solution
- Monthly Cost: R15–R170/month
- Includes: Website hosting, storage, automation, notifications
- Scales automatically with demand
- No additional staff required

### Option 2: Hiring Additional Staff
- Monthly Cost: R8,000–R15,000/month
- Includes: Salary for 1–2 additional employees
- Limited to working hours
- Still prone to human error

## Monthly Savings

- Minimum Savings: R7,830/month (R15,000 - R170 = R14,830... wait)
- Minimum Savings: R8,000 - R170 = R7,830/month
- Maximum Savings: R15,000 - R15 = R14,985/month
- Average Monthly Savings: R7,830–R14,830

## Annual Savings Projection

- Minimum Annual Savings: R7,830 x 12 = R93,960/year
- Maximum Annual Savings: R14,830 x 12 = R177,960/year

## AWS Cost Breakdown

- Amazon S3 (Website Hosting): ~R5–R50/month
- AWS Lambda (Email Automation): ~R0–R20/month
- Amazon DynamoDB (Database): ~R5–R50/month
- Amazon SNS (Notifications): ~R5–R50/month
- Total Estimated: R15–R170/month

## Why AWS is the Better Choice

1. Cost — 98% cheaper than hiring staff
2. Reliability — 99.99% uptime vs human availability
3. Scalability — handles 200+ customers without breaking
4. Accuracy — no more double bookings or order mix-ups
5. Speed — instant confirmations vs manual follow-ups
6. Data — centralised storage vs scattered paperwork

## Conclusion
The AWS solution delivers superior performance at a fraction of the cost. By investing R15–R170/month instead of R8,000–R15,000/month, The Unknown restaurant saves up to R177,960 annually while providing a better customer experience.
