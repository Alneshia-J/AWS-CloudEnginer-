The Unknown is a popular restaurant in Johannesburg serving over 200 customers daily. Despite its popularity, the restaurant faces multiple operational challenges due to rapid growth and reliance on manual systems.

## Key Problems Identified

### 1. Double Bookings and Order Mix-Ups
Manual reservation and ordering processes lead to frequent errors, causing customer frustration and lost revenue.

### 2. No Online Booking or Ordering System
Customers have no way to book a table or place an order online. Everything is handled via phone or in person, limiting accessibility.

### 3. No Automated Confirmations
Customers receive no confirmation after making a booking or placing an order, leading to uncertainty and no-shows.

### 4. No Centralised Data Storage
Customer information, bookings, and orders are scattered across paper records and spreadsheets with no single source of truth.

### 5. Costly Staff Expansion
To manage the growing demand manually, the restaurant would need to hire additional staff at R8,000–R15,000 per month — an unsustainable cost.

## Impact
These problems result in poor customer experience, operational inefficiency, and unnecessary costs that threaten the restaurant's ability to scale.
